Chicken Teriyaki Recipe


Background

Teriyaki is derived from the Japanese root words teri, to shine, and yaki, to broil or grill.
Thatâ€™s the way traditional teriyaki looks: shiny and incised with grill marks.
In Japan, teriyaki is a mix of soy sauce, sake and the rice wine mirin, which imparts a subtle sweetness.
The teriyaki found throughout Seattle, of which this is an adaptation, is a bit more showy.
Cooks sweeten with white sugar and pineapple juice. They thicken with cornstarch.
Ginger and garlic go into the mix because of the Korean ancestry of many cooks.
It is not at all traditional, but it is simple to prepare and addictive to eat.
Be sure to plan ahead as you do need to marinate the chicken before cooking.
An overnight stay in the fridge is ideal, but many readers have been happy with a quick marinade of an hour or so.


Ingredients

Yields 8 servings in 30 minutes (not counting marination)

  • 1 cup Soy Sauce
  • 1 cup Granulated Sugar
  • 1.5 teaspoon Brown Sugar
  • 6 cloves Garlic (minced or cruchsed)
  • 2 tablespoons Grated Ginger
  • 0.25 teaspoon Grounded Black Pepper
  • 3 inches of Cinnamon Stick
  • 1 tablespoon Pineapple Juice
  • 8 Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs
  • 2 tablespoons Cornstarch


Instructions

  1. In a small saucepan, combine all ingredients except cornstarch and chicken.
    Bring to boil over high heat before reducing heat to low and stirring until surgar dissolved.
    After 3 minutes remove saucepan from heat and let cool. Remove cinnamon stick and drizzle
    in about 0.5 cup of water.
  2. Place chicken in sealable ziploc bag. Add mixture from step one, seal, and let marinate.
    Marinate for at least 1 hour, but preferrably overnight.
  3. Remove chicken from marination and pour the marinade into saucepan.
    Bring to a boil again before reducing heat to low. Mix the cornstarch with a little
    bit of water and add to saucepan. Stir until thickened and add water to reach desired consistency.
    Set aside.
  4. Preheat a broiler or grill. Brush chicken with the sauce on all sides before
    boiling or grilling 3 mintes per side. While chicken is cooking, heat up the remaining sauce
    and set aside for serving. To serve, slick chicken into strips and drizzle with sauce.
